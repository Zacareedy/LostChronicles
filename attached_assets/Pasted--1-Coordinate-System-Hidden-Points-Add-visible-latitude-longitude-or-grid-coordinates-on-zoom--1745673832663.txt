 1. Coordinate System & Hidden Points
- Add visible latitude/longitude or grid coordinates on zoom.
- Allow players to input coordinates manually into the terminal.
- Some secrets (e.g., crash sites, caves, hidden bunkers) only appear when specific coordinates are entered, not by clicking normally.

👉 Example:  
Terminal Command: `goto 23.4N, 165.7W`  
- Map pans & zooms automatically to an otherwise unmarked spot.
- Maybe after a terminal confirmation, it reveals a minor marker.

---

 2. Dynamic Marker Discovery
- Not all markers are visible at the start.
- New stations or objects (like the Staff, The Looking Glass, the orchid) only appear:
  - After specific puzzles are solved
  - After reaching a new security level
  - At certain times (tied to the 108-minute cycle)


 3. Weather & Environmental Effects
- Trigger ambient changes on the map:  
  - Fog rolls in
  - Thunderstorms
  - Flickering lights on station markers
- Certain conditions could temporarily reveal hidden clues on the map.

👉 Example:  
During a storm event → Lightning flashes reveal a ruined station outline for 2 seconds.


4. Moving Entities or "Ghost" Signals
- Occasionally a moving dot appears — like a person or an animal.
- Players can track it via commands like `track.entity(id: 004)`.
- If followed or intercepted (by predicting next coordinates), it leads to finding new lore.

👉 Example:  
One moving dot is Radinsky before his suicide → track him to unlock Swan control room blueprints.

---

 5. Hidden Clickable Objects
- Parts of the environment itself (e.g., waterfalls, mountain tops, beach wreckage) are clickable hotspots.
- Clicking them without any hint is almost impossible — but terminal logs or decoded coordinates hint at their existence.

👉 Example:  
After interpreting a glyph that says "follow the water," you realize the river delta on the map is clickable.

---

 7. Time-Sensitive Map States
- Map changes subtly depending on the state of the world:
  - During a lockdown
  - When the countdown reaches certain multiples (e.g., every 27 minutes)
  - After system failures

👉 Example:  
- During system lockdown, some station names flicker → one shows an alternate name:  
  “The Swan” → briefly flickers as "The Hatch - Primary Field Site."

---

 8. Cross-Station Connection Visualization
- Once two or more stations are unlocked, a feature to draw connection lines.
- These could reveal hidden relationships:
  - Communication tunnels
  - Electrical grid links
  - Observation areas

👉 Example:  
- Connecting Swan → Flame → Pearl forms a triangle that hints at a central "missing" station.

---

 9. Station Log Summaries
- Clicking on a station could (later in progression) show a brief operational summary or maintenance logs.

👉 Example:  
- Click Swan → Log Summary:  
  "Status: Operational. Psychological protocol active. Minor power anomalies recorded 48h ago."

Later these could be used to solve puzzles ("Power anomaly means lockdown can be triggered manually").



 🔥 Bonus Interaction Concept:

Map Terminal Commands

Allow users to interact with the map via terminal, too:
- `map.reveal_hidden()`
- `map.trace(entity)`
- `map.scan(frequency: 72Mhz)`
- `map.analyze_topography(sector: C-7)`

Executing these could create little flickers or distortions that reward very observant players without making the map feel "video gamey" or obvious.
